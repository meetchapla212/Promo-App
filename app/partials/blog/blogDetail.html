A virtual happy hour is a social event that is held over video and social media platforms. You can use platforms like Zoom or Google Meet, or even more casual relaxed platforms like Facebook rooms.

The point of a virtual happy hour is really to have fun and to facilitate engagement between attendees. You can host a virtual happy hour for friends and family, but also for colleagues, or members of a team or club. Whoever you’re hosting the virtual happy hour for, there are a variety of fun activities you can come up with to boost engagement. By keeping everyone busy and entertained you will ensure that your virtual happy hour is absorbing and fun.

Virtual happy hours are usually very appropriate team-building activities. With working from home now being commonplace, and also teams working together from different locations, using virtual happy hours in this way is important to keep teams connected.

If you’re new to hosting virtual happy hours, and need a bit of guidance when it comes to planning out activities, and keeping the flow going, you have come to the right place.

Get the Ball Rolling

First things first. When you’re setting up a virtual happy hour, you have to give your potential attendees enough time in advance. They need to be able to clear their calendar or make sure that another meeting does not pop up.

The most effective way to ensure that everyone will be able to attend your virtual happy hour will be to send out an invitation that is linked to a calendar. One of the easiest ways to do this is to send a Zoom or google meet link via email. Your attendees will be able to RSVP and automatically save the information on their calendar. They will be reminded about the meeting in advance and you can make sure you have a full house in attendance.

If you fail to send your invitations in due time or fail to remind your attendees about the event, they may forget about it. Virtual happy hours are all about inclusion and building team spirit, so to have your attendees miss the event would be a real shame.
